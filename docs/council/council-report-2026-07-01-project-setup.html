Council Report

Project-setup connective layer — build vs compose, and delivery

5 advisors · anonymous peer review · chairman synthesis

The question

What shape should a "project-setup connective layer" take for prd-to-product's Claude Code skill ecosystem — how much to BUILD vs COMPOSE, and how to DELIVER it (plugin/marketplace vs vendored)? The skills' owner (Rex) is a designer who delegates ALL engineering judgment to Claude. A two-project review (seance, native iOS shipped; Strays, RN/Expo frozen at the trio→build seam) verified a recurring gap between the trio (prd-creator→context-engineering→design-system-bootstrap, which plans+scaffolds) and standing up stack/security/tests/deploy. Deep research found the market sells every individual CAPABILITY as maintained tools but NOBODY sells the per-project-type composition+verification GLUE. Guardrail from a prior council: capture durable QUESTIONS not perishable ANSWERS; composing externally-maintained tools is not freezing. Hard constraint: verification often can't run in the agent's shell (iOS CI industry-broken on Xcode 26), so it must route to a capable actor. Four forks: (1) BUILD vs COMPOSE the glue (borrowables: cruft linked-template drift model; verify-it-ran ladder = doctor probes + Terraform check + GitHub needs:-DAG); (2) DELIVERY plugin/marketplace vs vendored; (3) starter lane per stack; (4) iOS submission Fastlane vs Xcode Cloud.

The recommendation

Council verdict

COMPOSE the glue and VENDOR it now — but build nothing until you prove a specific human can actually read a red verification rung, because the whole routing design silently assumes a "capable actor" the premise says doesn't exist.

Compose, don't build. Vendor now, defer plugin/marketplace and forks #3/#4 with a decision rule. But treat the verification-routing table as an unproven hypothesis, not a settled design — and gate everything on one test first. I side with the majority on direction because it survives every objection: building glue is a maintenance business one non-engineer cannot staff, and vendoring is the reversible choice at N=2. But I weight the Skeptic's dissent heavily on mechanism, because four peer reviewers independently confirmed his two contradictions are real and unanswered. The synthesis: the durable question-set is the only thing that compounds, and it is worth authoring even if everything else fails — so author that first and let it prove whether a routing layer is real. Do not adopt cruft on faith; its drift-propagation may be incompatible with your own guardrail, and you can get the durable question checklist without it. Do not build the routing table as polished prose before a single instance has unfrozen a real frozen seam. The honest risk is that at N=2 this is a personal workflow dressed as a product; the discipline that keeps you honest is refusing to build the abstraction until one concrete instance earns it.

The one thing to do first

Before writing any glue, hand-write the verification-routing table for one real frozen seam (Strays' RN seam, or seance's iOS gap) — four columns: check / who judges it / what red looks like / what green looks like — then physically test whether Rex can read a deliberately-failed rung. If Rex looks at a red cruft check (or a failed doctor probe) and cannot tell whether it's actionable or noise, the routing terminates at a blind judge and the layer is theater — stop and redesign around CI-as-judge or don't build it. If it unfreezes the seam and Rex can read the signal, the layer is real and you build outward from that proven instance. This single test verifies premise #1 (a capable actor exists) and premise #3 (the glue is needed, not a hand-note substitute) — the two the whole recommendation depends on and no advisor established.

Where the council agrees

All five advisors converge, unusually tightly, on the two central forks. On BUILD vs COMPOSE it is unanimous: compose externally-maintained tools; author only the durable question-set and the verification-routing table. Every advisor independently reaches the same reason — a single non-engineer cannot staff the maintenance treadmill that building glue creates, the exact 'freeze perishable judgment' rot the prior council warned against. On DELIVERY, four of five say vendor now, plugin/marketplace later — the packaging tax only pays off at N-consumers and you have two projects; cruft check in CI is the drift alarm that makes vendoring survivable. Only the Stranger dissents toward plugin-first. On forks #3 and #4, unanimous: defer with a decision rule, favoring company-backed maintained options where Rex can't judge staleness (Tuist over XcodeGen, Xcode Cloud over Fastlane), because those pins depreciate on a 6–18 month clock. The council also agrees the routing table — not any tool — is the actual product.

Where the council clashes

Two real clashes, one fatal. First, delivery: the Stranger argues plugin-first because 'the plugin is the one channel where cruft check in CI can nag Rex,' while the majority argues vendor-first because plugins GA'd only nine months ago and conventions will churn — you'd freeze a distribution shape before you have usage. The majority is stronger: vendoring is reversible, plugin-later is a one-way door you can defer cheaply. Second, more serious: whether the shared recommendation is even coherent. The Skeptic stands alone against four on a structural objection the majority never answers: (a) the verify-it-ran ladder 'routes to the capable actor,' but the only actor delegates ALL engineering judgment — so the ladder terminates at someone who cannot tell a real red rung from noise; and (b) cruft's linked-template model propagates canonical template answers via cruft update, contradicting 'capture questions, not answers.' Four advisors adopt cruft AND endorse questions-not-answers without noticing these collide — the Veteran most sharply, naming concrete pins in the same breath as endorsing questions-only.

Blind spots the council caught

The peer reviews are damning and consistent — four of five reviewers independently flagged the Skeptic's two contradictions as the thing everyone else glided past. Three load-bearing, unverified premises emerge, and they undercut the majority verdict: (1) The routing table may terminate at a judgment-blind owner. Every advisor except the Skeptic treats 'route verification to the capable actor' as a solution without establishing a capable actor exists. If Rex cannot read a red rung, the ladder is unfalsifiable theater whether you build or compose. (2) 'Cruft propagates only shape/questions' is asserted, never verified — the compose-with-cruft consensus may rest on a mechanism that structurally cannot honor the guardrail. (3) 'Nobody sells the composition glue' is treated as verified whitespace by all five — and at N=2 that is equally consistent with 'the glue is unsellable/unneeded.' No advisor priced the abstraction against the honest baseline: writing per-project setup notes by hand, twice. Given three unverified load-bearing premises, confidence in the specific mechanism (cruft + routing table) is low-to-moderate; confidence in the direction (compose over build, vendor over plugin) stays high because it survives even if the premises fail — a hand-maintained checklist is still compose-not-build, still vendored.

Advisor responses

01 The Skeptic

The composition thesis is sound; the delivery mechanism is where this dies. The fatal flaw hides in Fork #1's own hard constraint: verification "must route to the capable actor — owner's eye, GUI, or CI," but the capable actor is a designer who delegates ALL engineering judgment to Claude. So the verify-it-ran ladder terminates at a human who cannot judge whether the rung passed. "Route to the owner's eye" is a null instruction when the eye can't read the signal. The iOS PTY wall isn't the real wall — the JUDGMENT wall is. Who confirms `cruft check` failing in CI means something actionable vs noise? Not Rex. So either CI becomes the judge (and now you're maintaining CI config — the exact perishable-pin rot the guardrail forbids), or the ladder is theater. Second: cruft's linked-template model contradicts the guardrail — `cruft update` propagates template CHANGES, meaning the template carries answers concrete enough to propagate (pins). You can't simultaneously (a) capture only durable questions and (b) run a drift-propagation mechanism — a question has nothing to propagate. Third: "nobody sells the glue" for a two-project single-designer sample isn't whitespace — it's a market of one; the composition surface changes faster than any single maintainer can track. You'd become the under-resourced Fastlane you're wary of. Next step: before ANY build, write down who judges each verification rung when red — by name. If it's "Rex" and Rex can't read it, stop.

02 The Architect

COMPOSE the glue; author only the composition manifest and the verification-routing table — nothing that pins a version. The whitespace isn't a capability gap, it's an ORCHESTRATION + JUDGMENT-ROUTING gap, so building tools would put you in a maintenance business you can't staff. Borrow cruft's linked-template model but bind it to EXTERNALLY-MAINTAINED templates (Obytes for RN, Tuist for iOS), not one you author — so `cruft update` pulls the maintainer's fixes, not Rex's rotting pins. The only thing you write is the durable QUESTION set and the verify-it-ran ladder. Reframe the hard constraint: the PTY wall isn't a bug to engineer around, it's the DEFINING FEATURE. The layer's real job is "route each verification to the actor who can actually judge it." That table (agent-shell / owner's-eye / GUI / CI) IS the product. Fork #2: vendored, decisively — plugins pay off at N-projects and multiple consumers; you have one developer. Vendoring drifts, but `cruft check` in CI turns drift into a visible signal. #3/#4: defer with a rule — company-backed maintained option when Rex can't judge staleness (Tuist over XcodeGen; Xcode Cloud over Fastlane). Biggest risk: the routing table ossifies into stale prose. Counter: at N=2 this is over-engineering a pattern not a product. Refuse to build until the table earns it. Next step: hand-write the verification-routing table for Strays' frozen seam — four columns, real checks. If it unfreezes Strays, the layer is real.

03 The Stranger

Compose, don't build. Deliver as a PLUGIN. Defer #3/#4. The load-bearing fact: "verify-it-ran ladder"/"PTY wall" means the agent literally cannot see whether what it set up works. The only user is Rex — a designer who cannot judge when an engineering pin is stale; his lived experience of a self-built glue layer is: works twice, then silently rots, no eye to catch it. BUILD is disqualified not on effort but on maintainability-by-this-specific-human. Compose externally-maintained tools (cruft for drift-binding, doctor-probes reporting to Rex's eye/CI, not the agent's shell). The durable thing you author is the question checklist per stack, never the answers. Delivery: plugin, because vendored copies drift and Rex can't see the drift; the plugin is the one channel where "cruft check in CI" can nag him. Biggest risk: even the composition layer names tools that die (Fastlane #29713). Counterargument: a checklist with no runnable teeth is just a nag Rex ignores. Next step: write the per-stack verification checklist — questions only, zero pins — test against Strays' frozen seam.

04 The Long View

Specific pins depreciate to zero on a 6–18 month clock; you'll be wrong about Fastlane-vs-Xcode-Cloud within a year regardless. What survives is the durable checklist and the routing. Author that; borrow everything executable. Second-order effect: if you BUILD the glue, you inherit a maintenance liability only you can service — a non-engineer maintaining perishable config is the exact rot the prior council warned against. Composing cruft + doctor-probes + CI DAGs means someone else eats the upgrade treadmill; that asymmetry decides fork #1 alone. Delivery: vendor now — plugins GA'd nine months ago, young, conventions will churn; vendoring is reversible and you gain real usage from seance/Strays before freezing a distribution shape. Adopt cruft's linked-template model so vendored copies aren't orphaned. Biggest risk: the verification-routing abstraction is genuinely hard and you'll under-build it. Counter: composition means N brittle external dependencies. Next step: write the durable question-set checklist for one stack (RN/Strays) — no pins.
