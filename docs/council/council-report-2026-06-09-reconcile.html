Council Report

Reconciling a Claude Design bundle PRD with an interviewed PRD — and should the council be a primitive?

5 advisors · anonymous peer review · chairman synthesis

The question

The prd-to-product skill kit reconciles two prose PRDs from different sources. The operator now prototypes most products in Claude Design first, which exports a 'handoff bundle' (tokens + PRD + screenshots + stack), becoming his default front door until/unless Anthropic integrates it natively into Claude Code. Prior decision D-008 built NO ingestion automation (N=1 schema-uncertainty); the kit already prevents silent clobbering via a write hook, so the open problem is JUDGMENT, not write-safety. CRUX: the bundle PRD often contains SUPERIOR features/layouts (because prototyping lets the operator SEE them), while the interviewed PRD is stronger on strategy/scope/rationale. Q1: which reconciliation mechanism wins — (a) human diff-and-adjudicate, (b) structured section-by-section merge protocol, (c) an LLM Council pass that drafts a reconciled PRD, (d) feed bundle PRD as source material into prd-creator's confirm-or-correct intake, (e) something else? Q2: should the LLM Council be embedded as a REUSABLE primitive wired into the kit's decision points, or stay ad hoc? Reflexivity: if the council can't earn its keep on THIS question, that's evidence against Q2.

The recommendation

Council verdict

Do human diff-and-adjudicate over a section-keyed delta view that routes deltas by KIND — mechanically absorb pure additions, force explicit adjudication on anything that re-expands cut scope — and keep the council ad hoc, not a wired-in primitive.

Side with the Skeptic/Veteran reading of (a), upgraded by the peer round. Do human diff-and-adjudicate, but make the mechanism do two things the raw option doesn't. (1) Map both PRDs onto prd-creator's section skeleton and surface per-section deltas — so this composes with the existing intake and the (a)/(d) distinction dissolves: you get the intake's in-the-loop machinery without building anything new. (2) Route deltas by KIND, not just by source. Pure additions (the bundle has a feature/layout the interview never spoke to) are presumptively safe — present them, let the operator wave them through. Anything that contradicts or re-expands a scope decision the interview made on purpose gets flagged as a scope-gate violation and held for explicit adjudication, framed as 'the bundle re-introduces something you decided to cut' — NOT 'the bundle has a better idea.' That reframing is the defense against self-adjudication bias: it makes the operator overrule a prior deliberate decision, which is harder to rubber-stamp than approving a shiny prototype. This protects the one thing the kit exists to protect — scope discipline — against the one threat this situation introduces, prototype enthusiasm laundering cut scope back in. It builds nothing structural, so it survives the front door being temporary. On Q2: no — keep the council ad hoc, hand-invoked at genuine forks where being wrong is expensive AND contested-across-people. This question barely cleared that bar; routine reconciliation won't. The reflexivity test is admittedly non-falsifiable as run (the prompt pre-selected a council-worthy question), but the external-plugin dependency and the babysitting requirement are independently sufficient to reject wiring it in. Hold one thing lightly: the screenshots, not the prose, carry the real 'this is superior' signal — don't over-invest in prose-merge tooling when the judgment is visual.

The one thing to do first

For this one bundle, by hand: lay the bundle PRD and the interview PRD side by side against prd-creator's section skeleton, and tag every divergence as either 'pure addition' or 'contradicts a deliberate scope cut' — then adjudicate only the second pile. Whether that tagging step is worth encoding into the kit is a decision you make after doing it three times, not now.

Strong, near-unanimous convergence on the high-stakes points. Where the council clashes

One real clash, narrowed by the peer round. The surface split is (a) human diff-and-adjudicate (Skeptic, Stranger, Veteran) vs. (d) feed the bundle through prd-creator's confirm-or-correct intake (Architect, Long View). But Review 4 nails it: (a) and (d) describe the same loop at different layers — surface deltas (a), absorb them through a section-keyed intake (d). The disagreement is largely illusory. The substantive clash is whether routing the bundle through the intake is SAFE: the Skeptic and Veteran argue (d) is actively dangerous because prd-creator's intake is built to TRUST its input, so feeding an un-adjudicated bundle through it launders deliberately-cut scope back in disguised as 'a better product idea.' The Architect and Long View pick (d) without reckoning with that — and the peer round flagged this against the Long View specifically.

Blind spots the council caught

The review round earned its keep — three gaps every advisor missed. (1) Two delta-kinds need different handling and nobody routed them: 'bundle adds a feature the interview missed' is mechanically safe to absorb, while 'bundle contradicts a scope decision made on purpose' is the trap and the ONLY kind that needs a human. All five collapsed these into one diff. This is the most actionable insight in the exercise — the judgment-layer analogue of the write-hook the kit already has. (2) The strongest signal may not be in the prose at all: the bundle is tokens + PRD + screenshots + stack, and the operator's 'superior' judgment comes from SEEING it — so the load-bearing evidence lives in the screenshots, and all five assumed prose-vs-prose was the right altitude. (3) Self-adjudication conflict of interest: the operator adjudicating is the same person who prototyped the bundle and felt the features were superior; 'human adjudicates' means asking the enthusiast to overrule his own fresh prototypes. Review 5 sharpens it — 'superior' is itself the contested judgment, so the real primitive is a SCOPE-GATE, not a source-of-authority tag.

Advisor responses

01 The Skeptic

Two PRDs each carrying superior content is not a merge problem, it's a provenance problem. The bundle PRD's strength (features you SAW work) and the interview PRD's strength (strategy, scope discipline) live at different altitudes — features vs. rationale. So (a) human diff-and-adjudicate wins, but only if the agent surfaces deltas tagged by KIND: "bundle adds a feature/layout the interview missed" vs. "bundle contradicts a scope decision the interview made on purpose." The second kind is the trap. The bundle has no scope discipline — Claude Design will happily prototype three features you decided to cut. A naive additive merge silently re-imports cut scope wearing the costume of "superior product idea." That is the fatal flaw in (b)/(c)/(d): any protocol that treats the bundle as co-equal input lets prototype enthusiasm overwrite deliberate restraint, and you won't notice because it LOOKS like a better product. So (d) is actively dangerous — feeding the bundle into prd-creator's confirm-or-correct intake launders un-adjudicated scope back through a flow designed to trust its input. On Q2: no. Embedding the council as a wired-in primitive on a single-dev markdown kit is ceremony that fails its own test. You convened a 5-advisor council to decide whether to convene councils — and the honest output is "use the cheap tool." Its first-pass overreached twice this week; a primitive that needs peer-review to not be confidently wrong is not a primitive, it's a liability with a hard dependency on an external marketplace plugin your kit can't assume exists. Keep it ad hoc, operator-invoked, for genuine forks. The threshold: council only when a wrong call is expensive AND irreversible. PRD reconciliation is neither — it's diff-and-adjudicate.

02 The Architect

Reframe first: you are not building "PRD reconciliation." You have two artifacts that are strong on orthogonal axes — the bundle is strong on what to build (features, layouts, product surface you can see), the interview is strong on why and how much (strategy, scope, rationale). The wrong variable to optimize is "merge them into one canonical PRD." The right variable is provenance: keep each section tagged by source-of-authority and only adjudicate where they actually collide. Q1: (d) wins, with a twist. Feed the bundle PRD as source material into prd-creator's existing confirm-or-correct intake — that machinery already exists, already surfaces deltas, already puts you in the loop. But pre-seed it: bundle owns the Features/Layouts/UX sections by default; interview owns Strategy/Scope/Rationale by default; the intake only stops you on genuine contradictions, not every difference. (a) is what (d) becomes at divergences. (c) council-drafts a PRD is the failure mode — it manufactures a third opinion you now have to reconcile against two. Q2: No. Wire the council in as a primitive and you've built ceremony into a single-dev kit whose entire thesis is lightweightness. The threshold is irreversibility × ambiguity. PRD reconciliation is reversible (it's prose, edit it) and the authority-split resolves most of it mechanically — below threshold. Reserve council for genuine forks: stack choice, the markdown-only invariant, "build vs. don't" like D-008. Reflexivity check, honestly: this question is exactly at the threshold — high ambiguity, but the decision (a confirm-or-correct intake) is cheap and reversible. It earned a council this once because the framing was contested. It would not earn a standing wire-in. That asymmetry is your answer to Q2.

04 The Long View

Two depreciation curves are about to cross, and the kit is being built on the wrong one. The Claude Design front door is the explicit reason this question exists, and the operator himself says it stays the default "until/unless Anthropic integrates Claude Design natively into Claude Code." That clause is the whole game. The moment native integration ships, any bespoke ingestion machinery — schema parsers, merge protocols, council-wired reconciliation — becomes dead weight you now have to detect, deprecate, and rip out. Software depreciates; the more you build against an external format you don't control, the faster. D-008's restraint wasn't timidity, it was correctly refusing to capitalize a wasting asset. That logic doesn't expire just because the bundle PRD turned out to be good. So on Q1: the winner is (d) — feed the bundle PRD as source material into prd-creator's existing confirm-or-correct intake. It compounds instead of depreciating, because you're investing in the interview muscle you keep regardless of whether the front door is Claude Design, the next tool, or a napkin. The interview is exactly the mechanism that's already strong on the things the bundle is weak on — strategy, scope, rationale — and it forces the operator to adjudicate the bundle's superior layouts rather than inherit them unexamined. (b) and (c) build format-specific scaffolding you'll throw away. On Q2: no. The council's value is that it's rare. Wire it into routine decision points and you convert judgment into ritual, train yourself to outsource the adjudication muscle you most need to keep sharp, and bolt a single-dev kit to an external marketplace plugin — a portability liability that ages badly. Keep it ad hoc, invoked at genuine forks. The reflexivity test confirms it: this question deserved a council. Reconciling next Tuesday's PRD will not.

05 The Veteran

Diff-and-adjudicate is the standard playbook here, and the standard playbook is right for the same reason it's always right: with N=1 bundle, you don't yet know the shape of the problem. Every team I've watched build a "merge protocol" before they had ten real merges built it for the wrong divergences and then maintained the wrong abstraction for a year. You have ONE bundle. Build nothing structural. So for Q1: (d) is the trap dressed as elegance — "feed the bundle PRD into prd-creator's intake" sounds clean but it launders judgment into a black box and re-runs an interview optimized for greenfield strategy, which is exactly the dimension the bundle is WORSE at. You'd dilute the bundle's superior layout/feature content through a strategy-first funnel. (a) wins, unconditionally, at N=1. The agent surfaces deltas section-by-section, you adjudicate per-divergence. That IS the structured protocol (b) — you just don't write it down as a protocol until the pattern repeats enough to name it. Failure mode of (a): adjudication fatigue on a 40-section diff. Mitigation: agent pre-labels each delta by which source is presumptively stronger (bundle=layout/features, interview=strategy/scope) so you're confirming, not deriving. Q2: No. And the tell is in your own reflexivity clause — the council overreached twice this week and only its peer-review caught it. A primitive you must babysit isn't a primitive. Hard external-plugin dependency on a single-dev markdown kit also breaks portability, which is the one invariant you've defended everywhere else. Keep the council ad hoc, invoked by hand at genuine forks. The threshold: council when the decision is irreversible AND contested across people; diff-and-adjudicate when it's one operator reconciling prose he can re-edit tomorrow.
