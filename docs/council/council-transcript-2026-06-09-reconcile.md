# Council transcript — 2026-06-09 — PRD reconciliation + council-as-primitive

## Framed question

The `prd-to-product` skill kit reconciles two prose PRDs from different sources. The operator now prototypes most products in Claude Design first, which exports a "handoff bundle" (tokens + PRD + screenshots + stack), becoming his default front door until/unless Anthropic integrates it natively into Claude Code. Prior decision D-008 built NO ingestion automation (N=1 schema-uncertainty); the kit already prevents silent clobbering via a write hook, so the open problem is JUDGMENT, not write-safety. CRUX: the bundle PRD often contains SUPERIOR features/layouts (because prototyping lets the operator SEE them), while the interviewed PRD is stronger on strategy/scope/rationale.

- **Q1 (reconciliation):** which mechanism wins — (a) human diff-and-adjudicate, (b) structured section-by-section merge protocol, (c) an LLM Council pass that drafts a reconciled PRD, (d) feed bundle PRD as source material into prd-creator's confirm-or-correct intake, (e) something else?
- **Q2 (council-as-primitive):** should the LLM Council be embedded as a REUSABLE evaluation step wired into the kit's decision points, or stay ad hoc?
- **Reflexivity:** if the council can't earn its keep on THIS question, that's evidence against Q2.

## Advisor responses (de-anonymized)

### The Skeptic
Two PRDs each carrying superior content is not a merge problem, it's a provenance problem. The bundle PRD's strength (features you SAW work) and the interview PRD's strength (strategy, scope discipline) live at different altitudes — features vs. rationale. So (a) human diff-and-adjudicate wins, but only if the agent surfaces deltas tagged by KIND: "bundle adds a feature/layout the interview missed" vs. "bundle contradicts a scope decision the interview made on purpose." The second kind is the trap. The bundle has no scope discipline — Claude Design will happily prototype three features you decided to cut. A naive additive merge silently re-imports cut scope wearing the costume of "superior product idea." That is the fatal flaw in (b)/(c)/(d): any protocol that treats the bundle as co-equal input lets prototype enthusiasm overwrite deliberate restraint, and you won't notice because it LOOKS like a better product. So (d) is actively dangerous — feeding the bundle into prd-creator's confirm-or-correct intake launders un-adjudicated scope back through a flow designed to trust its input. On Q2: no. Embedding the council as a wired-in primitive on a single-dev markdown kit is ceremony that fails its own test. You convened a 5-advisor council to decide whether to convene councils — and the honest output is "use the cheap tool." Its first-pass overreached twice this week; a primitive that needs peer-review to not be confidently wrong is not a primitive, it's a liability with a hard dependency on an external marketplace plugin your kit can't assume exists. Keep it ad hoc, operator-invoked, for genuine forks. The threshold: council only when a wrong call is expensive AND irreversible. PRD reconciliation is neither — it's diff-and-adjudicate.

### The Architect
Reframe first: you are not building "PRD reconciliation." You have two artifacts that are strong on orthogonal axes — the bundle is strong on what to build (features, layouts, product surface you can see), the interview is strong on why and how much (strategy, scope, rationale). The wrong variable to optimize is "merge them into one canonical PRD." The right variable is provenance: keep each section tagged by source-of-authority and only adjudicate where they actually collide. Q1: (d) wins, with a twist. Feed the bundle PRD as source material into prd-creator's existing confirm-or-correct intake — that machinery already exists, already surfaces deltas, already puts you in the loop. But pre-seed it: bundle owns the Features/Layouts/UX sections by default; interview owns Strategy/Scope/Rationale by default; the intake only stops you on genuine contradictions, not every difference. (a) is what (d) becomes at divergences. (c) council-drafts a PRD is the failure mode — it manufactures a third opinion you now have to reconcile against two. Q2: No. Wire the council in as a primitive and you've built ceremony into a single-dev kit whose entire thesis is lightweightness. The threshold is irreversibility × ambiguity. PRD reconciliation is reversible (it's prose, edit it) and the authority-split resolves most of it mechanically — below threshold. Reserve council for genuine forks: stack choice, the markdown-only invariant, "build vs. don't" like D-008. Reflexivity check, honestly: this question is exactly at the threshold — high ambiguity, but the decision (a confirm-or-correct intake) is cheap and reversible. It earned a council this once because the framing was contested. It would not earn a standing wire-in. That asymmetry is your answer to Q2.

### The Stranger
Reconciliation: (a) human diff-and-adjudicate is the right default, but not in raw form — it must be section-keyed. The bundle PRD and the interview PRD are not isomorphic prose, so a naive line diff produces noise. Map both onto prd-creator's existing section skeleton (problem, scope, features, layout, rationale), then surface per-section deltas. The operator decides each divergence. This wins because the crux you stated IS the answer: the two sources have known, predictable asymmetries — bundle wins on features/layout, interview wins on strategy/scope/rationale. That's not a judgment you discover fresh each time; it's a prior. Encode it. Option (d), feeding the bundle as source material into the intake, is the actual mechanism for HOW the surfaced deltas get absorbed — (a) and (d) compose, they don't compete. Failure mode of (a): operator rubber-stamps under fatigue and the interview's scope discipline silently erodes, which is the exact thing the kit exists to protect. Words a stranger can't parse: "scale-first token template," "value-lossless but structurally non-isomorphic," "confirm-or-correct intake," "N=1 schema-uncertainty." You're assuming the reader already believes the council is valuable — Question 2 asks whether it should be wired in, but the framing treats it as a primitive seeking a home. Council-as-primitive: no. You told me it overreached twice this week, caught only by peer review. A tool you must run in full to trust its first pass is not a primitive — it's a procedure. Reserve it for genuine forks where being wrong is expensive and reversible-cost is high. PRD reconciliation is neither; diff-and-adjudicate handles it. The reflexivity test bites: this question warranted a council; routine reconciliation does not.

### The Long View
Two depreciation curves are about to cross, and the kit is being built on the wrong one. The Claude Design front door is the explicit reason this question exists, and the operator himself says it stays the default "until/unless Anthropic integrates Claude Design natively into Claude Code." That clause is the whole game. The moment native integration ships, any bespoke ingestion machinery — schema parsers, merge protocols, council-wired reconciliation — becomes dead weight you now have to detect, deprecate, and rip out. Software depreciates; the more you build against an external format you don't control, the faster. D-008's restraint wasn't timidity, it was correctly refusing to capitalize a wasting asset. That logic doesn't expire just because the bundle PRD turned out to be good. So on Q1: the winner is (d) — feed the bundle PRD as source material into prd-creator's existing confirm-or-correct intake. It compounds instead of depreciating, because you're investing in the interview muscle you keep regardless of whether the front door is Claude Design, the next tool, or a napkin. The interview is exactly the mechanism that's already strong on the things the bundle is weak on — strategy, scope, rationale — and it forces the operator to adjudicate the bundle's superior layouts rather than inherit them unexamined. (b) and (c) build format-specific scaffolding you'll throw away. On Q2: no. The council's value is that it's rare. Wire it into routine decision points and you convert judgment into ritual, train yourself to outsource the adjudication muscle you most need to keep sharp, and bolt a single-dev kit to an external marketplace plugin — a portability liability that ages badly. Keep it ad hoc, invoked at genuine forks. The reflexivity test confirms it: this question deserved a council. Reconciling next Tuesday's PRD will not.

### The Veteran
Diff-and-adjudicate is the standard playbook here, and the standard playbook is right for the same reason it's always right: with N=1 bundle, you don't yet know the shape of the problem. Every team I've watched build a "merge protocol" before they had ten real merges built it for the wrong divergences and then maintained the wrong abstraction for a year. You have ONE bundle. Build nothing structural. So for Q1: (d) is the trap dressed as elegance — "feed the bundle PRD into prd-creator's intake" sounds clean but it launders judgment into a black box and re-runs an interview optimized for greenfield strategy, which is exactly the dimension the bundle is WORSE at. You'd dilute the bundle's superior layout/feature content through a strategy-first funnel. (a) wins, unconditionally, at N=1. The agent surfaces deltas section-by-section, you adjudicate per-divergence. That IS the structured protocol (b) — you just don't write it down as a protocol until the pattern repeats enough to name it. Failure mode of (a): adjudication fatigue on a 40-section diff. Mitigation: agent pre-labels each delta by which source is presumptively stronger (bundle=layout/features, interview=strategy/scope) so you're confirming, not deriving. Q2: No. And the tell is in your own reflexivity clause — the council overreached twice this week and only its peer-review caught it. A primitive you must babysit isn't a primitive. Hard external-plugin dependency on a single-dev markdown kit also breaks portability, which is the one invariant you've defended everywhere else. Keep the council ad hoc, invoked by hand at genuine forks. The threshold: council when the decision is irreversible AND contested across people; diff-and-adjudicate when it's one operator reconciling prose he can re-edit tomorrow. This question barely cleared that bar. Most won't.

## Chairman synthesis

**Recommendation headline:** Do human diff-and-adjudicate over a section-keyed delta view that routes deltas by KIND — mechanically absorb pure additions, force explicit adjudication on anything that re-expands cut scope — and keep the council ad hoc, not a wired-in primitive.

**Where the council agrees:** Q2 is a clean unanimous "no" on wiring the council in (ceremony on a lightweight kit, hard external-plugin dependency breaking portability, a "primitive" that needs peer review to not overreach is a procedure to babysit). No new structural machinery at N=1. The bundle=features / interview=scope asymmetry is a known prior to pre-label, not a fresh per-bundle discovery. Section-keyed, not raw diff.

**Where the council clashes:** Surface split (a) Skeptic/Stranger/Veteran vs. (d) Architect/Long View — but the peer round showed (a) and (d) are the same loop at different layers. The real clash: is routing the bundle through prd-creator's trust-its-input intake SAFE? Skeptic/Veteran say (d) is actively dangerous (launders cut scope); Architect/Long View pick (d) without reckoning with it.

**Blind spots caught:** (1) two delta-kinds need different handling and nobody routed them — pure additions are mechanically safe, scope-contradictions are the trap and the only kind needing a human; (2) the real "superior" signal is in the screenshots, not the prose; (3) self-adjudication conflict of interest — the operator adjudicating is the enthusiast who made the prototype, so the real primitive is a scope-gate, not a source tag.

**The recommendation:** Side with the Skeptic/Veteran reading of (a), upgraded: (1) map both PRDs onto prd-creator's section skeleton and surface per-section deltas (composes with the existing intake; the (a)/(d) distinction dissolves; nothing new built); (2) route deltas by KIND — pure additions are presumptively safe (wave through), but anything that contradicts/re-expands a deliberate scope cut is flagged as a scope-gate violation and held for explicit adjudication, framed as "the bundle re-introduces something you decided to cut," NOT "the bundle has a better idea." That reframing defends against self-adjudication bias. Protects scope discipline against prototype-enthusiasm laundering; builds nothing structural; survives the front door being temporary. Q2: no — keep the council ad hoc for forks that are expensive AND contested-across-people. Hold lightly: the screenshots carry the real signal; don't over-invest in prose-merge tooling.

**The one thing to do first:** For this one bundle, by hand — lay the bundle PRD and the interview PRD side by side against prd-creator's section skeleton, tag every divergence as "pure addition" or "contradicts a deliberate scope cut," and adjudicate only the second pile. Decide whether to encode the tagging step after doing it three times, not now.
